# A. Canonical Data Domains

| Serial Number | Data Domain Name | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Party | Encompasses all data related to individuals, organizations, and other legal entities. This includes identifiers, contact information, relationships, and demographic data, providing a single view of any entity interacting with the bank. |
| 2 | Product | Contains the master definition of all financial products and services offered by the institution. This includes features, pricing, eligibility criteria, and terms and conditions for products like loans, deposits, and investments. |
| 3 | Agreement | Represents the legal and binding contracts established between the bank and a party. This domain manages details of loan agreements, account opening contracts, and service level agreements, linking parties to the products they use. |
| 4 | Account | Manages all types of financial accounts, including deposit, credit, loan, and investment accounts. It holds information on account ownership, balances, status, and associated features, serving as a central hub for financial holdings. |
| 5 | Transaction | Captures the details of any financial or non-financial event that affects an account or party. This includes payments, transfers, withdrawals, deposits, and other significant business events with their complete lifecycle. |
| 6 | Financial Instrument | Covers data related to tradable financial assets such as securities, derivatives, and mutual funds. It includes instrument identifiers, pricing data, characteristics, and corporate actions information. |
| 7 | Collateral | Manages assets pledged by a party to secure a credit agreement. This domain tracks collateral details, valuations, liens, and the relationship between the collateral and the secured agreement. |
| 8 | Credit Risk | Focuses on the assessment, measurement, and management of credit-related risks. It includes credit scores, risk ratings, exposure calculations, covenant monitoring, and portfolio analysis data. |
| 9 | Financial Crime & Compliance | Contains data for detecting and preventing financial crimes like money laundering and fraud. This domain includes alerts, cases, watchlist screening results, and suspicious activity reporting (SAR) data. |
| 10 | Marketing & Campaign | Manages data related to marketing efforts, customer segmentation, and campaign execution. It tracks campaign performance, customer responses, leads, and marketing preferences (e.g., DNC). |
| 11 | Sales & Onboarding | Covers the end-to-end process of customer acquisition and product origination. This includes applications, underwriting, decisioning, and the setup of new accounts and services for a party. |
| 12 | Servicing & Operations | Pertains to the ongoing management and support of customer accounts and services. This includes customer service requests, account maintenance, statement generation, and operational workflows. |
| 13 | Collections & Recovery | Manages the process for handling delinquent accounts and recovering outstanding debt. It includes collection strategies, payment arrangements, charge-offs, and legal recovery actions. |
| 14 | Finance & Accounting | Includes the enterprise's internal financial records. This covers the general ledger, chart of accounts, sub-ledgers, financial statements (balance sheet, income statement), and budgeting/forecasting data. |
| 15 | Human Resources | Contains all data related to the workforce, including employee profiles, compensation, benefits, payroll, performance management, and recruitment. It manages the entire employee lifecycle. |
| 16 | Legal & Case Management | Manages legal matters, contracts, litigation, and other case-based work. This domain tracks case details, associated documents, deadlines, and billing from outside counsel. |
| 17 | Document & Content Management | Governs the storage, indexing, and lifecycle of all enterprise documents and unstructured content. This includes scanned images, electronic documents, reports, and customer communications. |
| 18 | Reference Data | Provides common, standardized data sets used for classification and consistency across the enterprise. This includes codes, enumerations, and master lists for countries, currencies, states, and industry classifications. |
| 19 | Analytics & Reporting | Consists of derived and aggregated data used for business intelligence, analytics, and reporting. It includes key performance indicators (KPIs), metrics, dashboards, and data marts. |
| 20 | IT & Infrastructure | Manages data about the organization's technology landscape. This includes information on software assets, hardware, applications, servers, user access controls, and IT service management. |
| 21 | Interaction | Captures records of all interactions between the bank and its parties across various channels. This includes call logs, email correspondence, web chats, and in-person meeting notes. |
| 22 | Location | Manages physical and geographical data. This includes addresses for parties and properties, branch and ATM locations, and geospatial data for analysis and reporting. |
| 23 | Regulatory Reporting | Contains data specifically prepared and formatted for submission to regulatory authorities. This includes reports for FinCEN, NMLS, and other governing bodies, ensuring compliance with legal requirements. |
